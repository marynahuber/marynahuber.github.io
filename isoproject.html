Duration

Jan 2021 - Jun 2022

Context & Challenge

The BISS payment system, interbank correspondence exchange, and cash exchange systems required a transition to ISO 20022 standards. This process was complicated by high uncertainty due to constantly changing government requirements and laws. Prioritizing tasks carefully was crucial to define a scope that was acceptable to both the customer (MTBank) and the development team.

My involvement and impact

  • Requirement Development:
    Led the creation of detailed requirements for receiving and processing XML messages, including payment requests, payment orders, and regulatory inquiries.
  • Collaboration:
    Worked closely with the National Bank (a government bank regulator), MTBank, a vendor company, and two diverse development teams to analyze and interpret regulatory requests. Ensured continuous communication to address any changes or concerns.
  • Data Mapping & Analysis:
    Conducted extensive data mapping and identified missing information crucial for generating accurate XML messages. Adapted existing systems to support new data fields and formats.
  • Algorithm Development:
    Designed and developed new algorithms to handle the processing of incoming and outgoing XML messages, ensuring compliance with strict ISO 20022 standards.
  • Testing & QA:
    Played an active role in acceptance testing with the regulator, focusing on verifying compliance and identifying any issues before full deployment.
  • Prioritization & Scope Definition:
    Prioritized tasks and defined the project scope to balance customer needs and development team capabilities.

Outcome

The project was completed on time, with the systems successfully transitioning to ISO 20022 standards. MTBank expressed their appreciation by sending a letter of thanks to the development team. The project provided valuable experience in managing uncertainty, regulatory compliance, system integration, and quality assurance.
